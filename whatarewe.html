InterVine is a Social Purpose Corporation operated by three women who have a passion for promoting sustainable, ethical social change and community development.


Meet the Team



Rachel Long:

Rachel is a senior Global Development major with an emphasis in Middle Eastern Studies. She has worked with both for-profits and non-profits over the years and has experienced the lack of effective, efficient collaboration between these two sectors. After living in the Middle East and working with refugee populations who became her close friends, she has been struggling with what more she could do to help her friends who were in dire situations. Over the past year and a half Rachel has had many conversations with people across the board and has realized the generosity that exists in our world and all the places, people, and causes that deserve this generosity. This is where the idea for InterVine started.


Danielle Meier:

Danielle is a senior Global Development and Political Science major with minors in Reconciliation Studies and Women’s Studies. Danielle has a deep passion for women’s issues within a global sociopolitical context and is excited to see the impact InterVine could have in the social change partnerships that are possible! She has witnessed firsthand the resource scarcity and unnecessary turnover in non-profits that greatly impede the organization’s ability to affect change. As the daughter of a small business owner, she also has experience in the for-profit world seeing the various ways a small company can engage with the community and some of the ways the current process and opportunities are lacking. Danielle believes in InterVine’s model and is thrilled to be changing the way we do change.


Ana Robin:

Ana is a senior Global Development major with a passion for community development through a grass-roots, relational approach. She has experience working for both for-profit businesses and non-profit organizations through an operational lens, and understands the importance that scarce resources play in mission fulfillment. Ana is eager to apply InterVine's model to businesses and non-profit organizations both within and beyond the Seattle community with the intention of fostering long-lasting, impactful partnerships that will ultimately provide the community with sustainable model of change.
